Photography

Over 100,000 photos captured and countless memories preserved.

I am currently the Head of Digitalization and Corporate Communication Secretariat in the College Representative Council of Sri Rajang College, Universiti Putra Malaysia Bintulu Campus. I manage all incoming photos from college-organized programs for documentation and publication on the college and university’s social media pages, and sometimes in newspapers. A notable program I photographed was UPM’s 48th Convocation, Session 1. Alongside that, I have documented many events within the college, university, and community programs such as longhouse visits.

I was also the Head of the Media Club at REAL Cheras during my secondary education. I managed over 100,000 photos taken by my team of photographers, ensuring every event was properly documented and shared for marketing, reporting, and memory-keeping purposes.

I ensured all photos were properly organized, edited, and filtered before being published. This process helped keep an easy-to-navigate internal directory for school media while ensuring the best quality images were shared.

Video Directing

Creating videos with meaning, memories, and impact.

Occasionally, I take on video projects to convey meaningful messages. My most notable projects include the Teacher's Day Celebration 2021 and my Graduation Message Video.

In 2021, during the lockdowns, a friend and I created a special Teacher’s Day video to show appreciation for our teachers who had been teaching us virtually. The video featured students turning on their cameras and sharing words of encouragement—something that made this moment unique to that year.

My Graduation Message Video was the last video I made for my school. It was a heartfelt project where my classmates shared their best memories and experiences. The interviews were unscripted to keep them authentic, allowing everyone to genuinely express their emotions.

Graphic Design

Designing visuals that bring programs and memories to life.

From time to time, I engage in graphic design—mainly focusing on creating posters, slides, and other visual materials for events. Whether it’s a promotional poster or an informative slide deck, I aim to design visuals that are both aesthetically appealing and effective in delivering key messages. My slides serve as engaging backdrops during presentations, helping to enhance storytelling and ensure that audiences receive information in a clear, structured, and visually compelling manner.

One of my most significant projects was designing my school’s yearbook. This involved compiling and curating a collection of photos, essays, and memories that captured the essence of our batch’s journey. In addition to the standard class and event pages, I incorporated special sections featuring heartfelt messages, student reflections, and a nostalgic look back at previous years. The final product spanned over 100 pages and was carefully designed to balance visual appeal with meaningful content. It was made available both in print and as a free digital copy, ensuring that every batchmate had access to this keepsake.

Beyond these projects, I continue to explore new design tools and techniques, refining my ability to communicate ideas through visuals. Whether it’s branding an event or crafting digital content, I enjoy the challenge of translating concepts into designs that leave a lasting impact.

Technical Work

Setting up and managing sound, lighting, and event technology.

I specialize in technical event work, handling the setup and management of sound systems, microphones, projectors, and lighting. Whether it’s a small presentation or a large-scale performance, I ensure that all audiovisual elements run smoothly. During high-stakes events, I take charge of the control room, coordinating live audio and visuals to create a seamless experience for both performers and audiences. I enjoy troubleshooting technical issues on the fly and making quick adjustments to maintain the flow of an event.

One of my most challenging projects was a primary school mini-musical, where I managed 10 microphones, including four head mics. This required careful audio balancing, muting mics when students were offstage, and ensuring clear sound for every performer. I meticulously planned the wiring, labeled each connection, and created a system to prevent technical failures. In addition to audio, I also handle stage lighting design, programming 20 programmable LED lights in my school hall to enhance stage presence and set the right atmosphere for different performances.

Currently, I am expanding my expertise by exploring digital mixers and live video streaming at my university. Learning advanced audio processing and real-time broadcasting allows me to stay updated with modern event production techniques. My goal is to refine my technical skills and apply them to increasingly complex events, ensuring every production runs at a professional standard.
